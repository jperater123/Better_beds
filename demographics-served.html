Demographics Served

Individuals with Disabilities and Behavioral Health Needs

Better Beds Transitional Housing provides supportive environments for individuals with intellectual disabilities, cognitive challenges, and behavioral health conditions. We partner with healthcare providers and case managers to ensure residents receive the support they need while working toward greater independence.

Our transitional housing offers:

  • Accessible facilities and accommodations
  • Coordination with healthcare and mental health services
  • Life skills training adapted to individual abilities
  • Supportive community environment
  • Assistance connecting to long-term housing and support services

Contact us to learn more about how we can support your transition to independent living.

Specialized Support Services

We understand that individuals have unique needs and circumstances. Our transitional housing can accommodate residents with various requirements, including:

  • Visual or hearing impairments
  • Chronic health conditions requiring ongoing care coordination
  • Mobility challenges and accessibility needs
  • Developmental disabilities
  • Activities and programs that promote social, emotional, and physical well-being

Veterans Transitional Housing

Supporting Those Who Served

We are honored to serve veterans transitioning to civilian life or facing housing instability. Our veteran-focused transitional housing provides a supportive environment that honors their service while addressing their unique transition needs.

Our veteran support services include:

  • Transitional housing for veterans returning to civilian life
  • Support for veterans with disabilities
  • Assistance accessing VA benefits and healthcare
  • Employment and career transition support
  • Peer support and veteran community connections
  • Trauma-informed care and mental health resources

Seniors and Older Adults

We provide transitional housing options for seniors who need temporary support during life transitions, such as recovery from illness, loss of housing, or while waiting for permanent assisted living placement.

Support for seniors includes:

  • Safe, accessible housing with senior-friendly features
  • Coordination with healthcare providers
  • Assistance accessing senior services and benefits
  • Social engagement opportunities
  • Support transitioning to permanent senior housing

Recovery and Sober Living

Our sober living transitional housing provides a structured, substance-free environment for individuals in recovery. We support residents as they build the skills and stability needed for long-term sobriety and independent living.

Recovery support includes:

  • Substance-free, accountable living environment
  • Peer support and recovery community
  • Connections to counseling and treatment providers
  • Life skills and relapse prevention education
  • Employment assistance and job readiness training
  • Support transitioning to permanent housing

Reentry and Second Chance Housing

We provide transitional housing for individuals reentering society after incarceration. Our program offers the stable foundation needed to successfully reintegrate into the community, reconnect with family, and build a positive future.

Reentry support includes:

  • Safe, stable transitional housing
  • Case management and reentry planning
  • Employment assistance and job placement
  • Life skills training and financial literacy
  • Access to legal aid and documentation services
  • Family reunification support
  • Mentorship and peer support

Families, Singles, and Homeless Prevention

Better Beds Transitional Housing serves individuals and families facing housing insecurity, homelessness, or economic hardship. We provide affordable transitional housing that creates stability while residents work toward long-term housing solutions.

We serve:

  • Families with children facing housing loss
  • Single adults transitioning from homelessness
  • Working individuals needing affordable housing
  • Those at risk of homelessness due to financial hardship
  • Anyone committed to achieving housing stability and independence

Our goal is to provide the support and resources needed to help every resident secure permanent, stable housing and achieve self-sufficiency.
